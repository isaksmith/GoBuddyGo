Product Requirement Document (PRD) Outline: GoBabyGo Buddy-Link ARThis document outlines the requirements for an AR application designed to foster inclusive play between a sibling and a child using a WSU GoBabyGo modified vehicle.1. Executive SummaryVision: To transform the "observer" role of a sibling into an active, gamified "co-pilot" experience that enhances safety and engagement without distracting the driver.Target Audience: Siblings (ages 4+) of children receiving cars through the WSU GoBabyGo program.2. User Roles & InteractionsRoleResponsibilityApp InteractionThe DriverSafely operating the GoBabyGo car.Minimal (Focus remains on the physical "Big Red Button").The Buddy (Sibling)Navigation, "maintenance," and scouting.Primary user of the AR interface on tablet or phone.3. Functional RequirementsA. Vehicle Recognition & TrackingThe app must utilize AR markers (e.g., a specific GoBabyGo sticker) to anchor digital assets to the physical car in real-time.Must maintain "World Tracking" so digital trails stay pinned to the floor as the car moves.B. The "Interactive Station" FeaturesVirtual Pit Stop: Interactive 3D overlays on the car’s wheels and battery compartment that react to "tapping" for simulated repairs.Pathfinder: A tool allowing the sibling to draw a "glowing path" on the ground in AR, providing a visual guide for the driver to follow.Resource Management: A "Fuel Gauge" that fills up when the sibling performs physical movement (e.g., "Run in place to generate power for the car!").C. Safety ProtocolsProximity Alert: If the sibling's device detects they are within 3 feet of the car's path, the screen must display a "Safety Stop" warning.No-Distraction Zone: AR elements must hover behind or beside the driver’s line of sight to ensure the driver’s attention remains on their path.4. Technical SpecificationsPlatform: iOS (ARKit) and Android (ARCore).Educational Integration: Feature a "Schematic View" derived from the Build Instructions to show how the car’s electrical components work.
